Cookies Policy

Transparent information about how we use cookies and similar technologies

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small data files (typically 4KB or less) stored on your browser or device when you visit websites. They contain unique identifiers and various data that websites use to enhance your browsing experience. Cookies are a standard technology used across the internet and are essential for the functioning of many websites. This Cookies Policy is compliant with GDPR, CCPA, ePrivacy Directive, and other cookie and privacy regulations.

2. Types of Cookies We Use

Essential/Strictly Necessary Cookies: These cookies are absolutely necessary for the website to function properly. They enable core functionality such as security, network management, accessibility, load balancing, user authentication, and session management. These cookies cannot be disabled without significantly impairing website functionality.

Functional Cookies: These cookies remember your preferences, settings, and choices to provide a personalized experience. Examples include language preferences, timezone settings, accessibility options, and saved form data. Functional cookies enhance usability but are not strictly necessary.

Performance/Analytics Cookies: These cookies collect aggregated information about how users interact with the website, including pages visited, time spent, click patterns, error messages, and navigation flows. This data helps us understand user behavior, identify technical issues, and improve website performance. We use Google Analytics and similar analytics services.

Marketing/Advertising Cookies: These cookies track your online activities across multiple websites to deliver targeted advertising based on your interests and browsing history. They measure the effectiveness of advertising campaigns and help advertisers understand audience demographics and behavior.

Third-Party Cookies: Third-party service providers (analytics firms, advertisers, social media platforms) may place cookies on your device. These cookies are subject to their respective privacy policies and terms.

3. Specific Cookies We Use

Session Cookies: Temporary cookies that expire when you close your browser. They maintain user session information.

Persistent Cookies: Long-term cookies that remain on your device for months or years to remember preferences and maintain user identity.

Common cookies we use include:

  • Google Analytics (_ga, _gid, _gat) - for website analytics
  • Authentication cookies - for login and session management
  • Preference cookies - for language, timezone, and accessibility settings
  • Security cookies - for fraud detection and security
  • Marketing cookies - for campaign tracking and conversion measurement

4. How We Use Cookie Information

  • To authenticate users and maintain secure sessions
  • To remember user preferences and settings
  • To understand website usage patterns and optimize performance
  • To track conversion funnels and marketing campaign effectiveness
  • To deliver personalized content and recommendations
  • To detect and prevent fraud and security threats
  • To provide analytics and insights into user behavior
  • To serve targeted advertisements based on interests
  • To conduct A/B testing and user experience optimization

5. Third-Party Cookies and Services

Our website uses several third-party services that place cookies on your device:

  • Google Analytics: Tracks website traffic and user behavior. Privacy Policy: https://policies.google.com/privacy
  • Google Ads/AdSense: Displays targeted advertisements. Privacy Policy: https://policies.google.com/privacy
  • Social Media Platforms: Facebook, LinkedIn, Twitter may place cookies for tracking and retargeting
  • Email Marketing Platforms: Services like Mailchimp or similar may use cookies for campaign tracking

We do not control third-party cookies and encourage you to review their privacy policies. These services are GDPR and CCPA compliant.

6. Consent and Cookie Preferences

When you first visit our website, you will see a cookie consent banner. You can:

  • Accept all cookies
  • Reject non-essential cookies
  • Access detailed cookie preferences and selectively enable/disable cookie categories
  • Withdraw or change your preferences at any time through the cookie settings

Essential cookies will remain enabled regardless of your preferences, as they are necessary for website functionality and security.

7. Cookie Duration and Expiration

Session Cookies: Expire automatically when you close your browser (typically minutes to hours)

Persistent Cookies: Remain on your device for extended periods:

  • Analytics cookies: 2 years
  • Preference cookies: Variable, typically 1-3 years
  • Marketing cookies: 30 days to 2 years depending on purpose

8. Managing and Deleting Cookies

Browser Settings: You can control cookies through your browser preferences:

  • Chrome: Settings > Privacy and Security > Cookies and other site data
  • Firefox: Preferences > Privacy & Security > Cookies and Site Data
  • Safari: Preferences > Privacy > Cookies and website data
  • Edge: Settings > Privacy, search, and services > Clear browsing data

Cookie Management Tools: Use tools like Your Online Choices (http://www.youronlinechoices.com) to manage advertising cookies across websites.

Do Not Track: Some browsers support Do Not Track (DNT) signals. We are not currently configured to respond to DNT signals, but you can disable cookies manually.

9. Impact of Disabling Cookies

Disabling essential cookies will impair website functionality and may prevent you from:

  • Logging into user accounts
  • Using secure features and transactions
  • Maintaining your session
  • Accessing certain website features

Disabling non-essential cookies will limit personalization but allow basic website access.

10. Privacy and Data Protection

Cookie data is treated as personal information and is subject to our Privacy Policy. We comply with GDPR, CCPA, PDPB, ePrivacy Directive, and other applicable regulations regarding cookie use and data collection. We do not sell cookie data to third parties and maintain strict data protection standards.

11. Do We Use Tracking Pixels or Beacons?

In addition to cookies, we may use web beacons (transparent 1x1 pixel images) and similar technologies for tracking purposes. These are used in emails and on web pages to monitor click-through rates, delivery confirmation, and campaign effectiveness. These technologies operate similarly to cookies in terms of data collection and privacy implications.

12. Targeted Advertising and Retargeting

We may use cookies to serve targeted advertisements based on your interests and browsing history across the internet. If you prefer not to receive targeted ads, you can opt-out through your browser settings, ad preference centers, or privacy tools like Your Online Choices or the Network Advertising Initiative (NAI).

13. Security of Cookie Data

We implement industry-standard security measures to protect cookie data from unauthorized access, alteration, or misuse. However, no security system is completely impenetrable, and we cannot guarantee absolute security of data transmitted over the internet.
